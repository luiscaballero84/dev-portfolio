Building the foundation for an efficient workflow and a future design system.

Starting from scratch

When I joined Raus, there was no organization of design assets in Figma – we didn't even have a company Figma account. As the first and only designer at Raus, it wasn't surprising. Given the huge amount of work required to create a design system, building one wasn't a top priority.

However, I knew I needed to establish some basic brand elements to make my workflow more efficient. These could serve as the groundwork for a complete design system in the future. We got a Figma account and I established a way to organize our files.

Starting with color and type

At that time, the website was the only source of truth for design elements. So, I carefully examined the code and extracted key variables such as colors and typography, which I then translated into Figma styles.

Brand Illustrations: vectorizing for versatility

Our brand illustrations, created by the talented Olga Prader, are crucial assets for our branding. They have a unique and cool vibe that helps us to differentiate ourselves from our competitors.

But there was a small challenge: they were in a non-vector format, which could be impractical for future uses and on the web. Luckily, the illustration were provided in large tiff files.

I took the opportunity to vectorize these illustrations and also saved them as components in Figma. Now, with a simple drag and drop, the illustrations are readily available for us to use.

Bringing uniformity with a new icon library

Another challenge I encountered was the lack of a unified icon library. Whenever a new icon was required, it was sourced from a different random collection. As a result, our icons ended up with inconsistent styles.

To solve this, we decided to implement a new unified icon library. Given the time constraints, we searched for an existing library that met two key criteria: a vast collection of high-quality icons and a style similar to our current icons.

We settled on the Google Materials Symbols icon library. With over 2500 well-designed glyphs and a handy Figma plugin, it is incredibly easy and fast to use.

The next steps in this ongoing project is to implement the new Figma variables feature for spacing and border radius.
